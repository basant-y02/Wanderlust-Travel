Santorini, Greece

Santorini is one of the Cyclades islands in the Aegean Sea. It was devastated by a volcanic eruption in the 16th century BC, forever shaping its rugged landscape.

The whitewashed, cubiform houses cling to cliffs above an underwater caldera. The island's beaches have black, red and white lava pebbles from its volcanic past.

Highlights:
  • Sunset views from Oia village
  • Red Beach with volcanic cliffs
  • Ancient ruins at Akrotiri
  • Local Assyrtiko wine tasting
  • Caldera boat tours

Kyoto, Japan

Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is known for its classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines and traditional wooden houses.

It's also known for formal traditions such as kaiseki dining and geisha entertainers found in the Gion district. Cherry blossoms in spring and colorful leaves in autumn make Kyoto especially beautiful.

Highlights:
  • Fushimi Inari Shrine's torii gates
  • Golden Pavilion (Kinkaku-ji)
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Forest
  • Traditional tea ceremonies
  • Gion district geisha culture

Bali, Indonesia

Bali is an Indonesian island known for its forested volcanic mountains, iconic rice paddies, beaches and coral reefs. The island is home to religious sites like the cliffside Uluwatu Temple.

Bali's culture is deeply spiritual, with daily offerings and elaborate temple ceremonies. The island offers everything from luxury resorts to backpacker hostels, surf spots to tranquil yoga retreats.

Highlights:
  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces
  • Uluwatu Temple cliff views
  • Ubud's art and culture
  • Seminyak's beaches and nightlife
  • Mount Batur sunrise trek

Paris, France

Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine.

Beyond landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is known for its cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

Highlights:
  • Eiffel Tower and Seine River cruises
  • Louvre Museum and Mona Lisa
  • Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur
  • Champs-Élysées and Arc de Triomphe
  • World-class dining and patisseries

Machu Picchu, Peru

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel located in the Eastern Cordillera of southern Peru on a 7,970 ft mountain ridge. It is the most familiar icon of Inca civilization.

The Incas built the estate around 1450 but abandoned it a century later at the time of the Spanish conquest. Although known locally, it was not known to the Spanish during the colonial period and remained unknown to the outside world until American historian Hiram Bingham brought it to international attention in 1911.

Highlights:
  • Sun Gate (Inti Punku) sunrise views
  • Temple of the Sun and Room of the Three Windows
  • Inca Bridge trail
  • Huayna Picchu mountain climb
  • Sacred Valley tours

Cape Town, South Africa

Cape Town is a port city on South Africa's southwest coast, on a peninsula beneath the imposing Table Mountain. Slowly rotating cable cars climb to the mountain's flat top, from which there are sweeping views of the city, the busy harbor and boats heading for Robben Island.

The island, once a notorious prison, is now a living museum. The city has some of South Africa's best restaurants, art galleries, beaches and wine regions nearby.

Highlights:
  • Table Mountain Aerial Cableway
  • Cape of Good Hope and penguins at Boulders Beach
  • Robben Island tours
  • Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden
  • Stellenbosch wine region tours
